# Apple Inc (AAPL)

## Investment Thesis
Apple is transitioning from a hardware upgrade-cycle story to an AI-platform story. The installed base of ~2.2B active devices gives Apple a distribution advantage no pure-play AI company can match. Services revenue (now ~25% of total) carries 70%+ gross margins and compounds on the device base. The key question is whether Apple Intelligence drives a meaningful iPhone upgrade super-cycle or remains a feature-parity play that merely defends share. We lean toward the latter near-term but see optionality in health, spatial computing, and on-device AI inference that the market underweights at current multiples.

## Business Model
- **iPhone** (~50% of revenue): Flagship hardware, drives ecosystem lock-in
- **Services** (~25%): App Store, iCloud, Apple Music, Apple TV+, AppleCare, licensing (Google TAC)
- **Mac** (~10%): Apple Silicon refresh cycle, professional and education markets
- **iPad** (~7%): Tablets, increasingly positioned for creative and enterprise use
- **Wearables, Home & Accessories** (~8%): Watch, AirPods, Vision Pro, HomePod

## Key Metrics to Track
- iPhone unit shipments and ASP trends (quarterly, IDC/Canalys for units, Apple for revenue)
- Services revenue growth and gross margin (target: sustain 14%+ growth, 70%+ margins)
- Installed base growth (active devices, reported annually — last: ~2.2B)
- Greater China revenue trajectory (macro sensitivity + Huawei competitive pressure)
- Apple Intelligence adoption / feature engagement (when disclosed — watch for WWDC metrics)
- Capital return program: buyback pace ($90B+ authorized) and dividend growth
- Gross margin trajectory (product vs. services mix shift)
- R&D spend as % of revenue (watch for step-up signaling new product categories)

## Competitive Position
Apple's moat is the ecosystem. Switching costs are high once users are embedded in iCloud, iMessage, AirDrop, and the health stack. In AI, Apple lags Google and Samsung on cloud-model capabilities but leads on privacy positioning and silicon efficiency (Neural Engine, M-series). The risk is that AI shifts the value from the device to the model provider, commoditizing hardware over time.

## Valuation Context
- Market cap ~$3.5T (May 2026), trading at ~32x forward P/E
- Market implies mid-single-digit revenue growth and continued margin expansion through Services mix
- Reverse DCF suggests market expects ~7% revenue CAGR through 2030 — achievable if Services compounds at 12%+ and iPhone holds flat
- Premium to peers justified by capital return program and ecosystem durability, but leaves little room for AI disappointment

## Key Risks
1. **AI feature parity gap**: Google/Samsung ship more capable on-device models; Apple Intelligence perceived as behind, reducing upgrade urgency
2. **China revenue pressure**: Huawei Mate resurgence + geopolitical risk erode Apple's premium position in Greater China
3. **Regulatory headwinds**: EU DMA forces App Store sideloading, potentially compressing Services margins over time
4. **Google TAC dependency**: Google search deal (~$20B/yr) faces DOJ antitrust scrutiny; any restructuring hits high-margin revenue directly
5. **Spatial computing uncertainty**: Vision Pro adoption slower than expected; unclear path to mass-market AR/VR

## Open Questions
- Will Apple Intelligence drive a measurable upgrade super-cycle in FY2027, or is it table-stakes?
- How does the Google search deal evolve post-DOJ ruling? What's the margin impact of alternative structures?
- Can Apple's on-device inference approach match cloud-based AI quality as models scale?
- What's the real TAM for Vision Pro / spatial computing at a sub-$2,000 price point?
- When does Apple disclose Apple Intelligence engagement metrics in a way that's investable?
- What does the "Baltra" server chip mean for Apple's cloud AI cost structure if it ships?
- How much incremental DRAM does the next iPhone need for on-device AI, and does it change BOM economics?
